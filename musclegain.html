Muscle Gain


What is a muscle gain diet?

Building muscle requires a positive energy balance,which means that you must take in more calories than you burn. You need roughly 2,800 calories to build a pound of muscle, largely to support protein turnover, which can be elevated with training.

In order to build muscles not only you should have the right diet plan but also target all your muscle groups at least 2 to 3 times a week to see expected results. For better results it is adviced to include a muscle gain supplments in your diet (which you can find on our supplements section).

Breakfast

  • 3-4 slices of whole wheat bread toast with peanut butter + 3 egg whites
  • or 1 cup low fat milk + 150g oatmeal + 1 banana(optional walnuts or almonds)

Mid Morning Snack

  • 1 apple + 1 orange
  • or 1 cup green tea + 2-3 multigrain biscuits (e.g Provita)

Lunch

  • 150g brown rice or whole wheat bread + 150g chicken/fish + salad or any other vegetables

Mid Afternoon Snack

  • 1 fruit + green tea/coffee or nuts

Dinner

  • 100g chicken/prawn/fish + stir fried vegetables + 1 cup brown rice or whole wheat bread
  • Bed time (optional)- 1 cup skimmed milk + 2 multigrain biscuits
